# Resume & LinkedIn Optimization Context

## Goal

This document summarizes all the decisions, design principles, and conclusions reached while optimizing my resume and LinkedIn profile.

Future improvements should **continue from this point** instead of starting over.

The objective is to maintain consistency across my Resume, LinkedIn profile, cover letters, and any future personal branding.

---

# Professional Positioning

The most important decision made during this optimization process was changing my positioning.

## Old positioning

> Flutter Developer

or

> Senior Flutter Developer

## New positioning

> Senior Software Engineer specialized in Flutter

This wording better represents my experience and opens opportunities beyond Flutter-specific searches.

It communicates that Flutter is my specialization—not the limit of my engineering capabilities.

This wording should be used consistently across:

* Resume
* LinkedIn
* Cover Letters
* Personal Website
* Portfolio
* Professional Bio

---

# Professional Identity

The resume should position me as someone who naturally operates as a:

* Senior Software Engineer
* Lead Flutter Engineer
* Lead Mobile Engineer

instead of simply someone who writes Flutter code.

The resume should communicate engineering maturity rather than framework expertise.

---

# Resume Philosophy

The objective is NOT to sound impressive.

The objective is to sound:

* authentic
* technically mature
* credible
* experienced
* human

Every sentence should feel like it was written by an experienced software engineer.

Avoid sounding like marketing.

Avoid sounding like AI.

Avoid exaggerated claims.

---

# Writing Style

The resume should always use:

* concise language
* technical language
* ownership language
* engineering terminology

Avoid:

* buzzwords
* corporate language
* AI clichés
* repetitive verbs
* generic wording

The final document should read naturally.

---

# Leadership Positioning

The resume should consistently communicate:

* technical leadership
* ownership
* architecture
* mentoring
* release management
* planning
* engineering standards
* delivery ownership
* collaboration

Whenever supported by the experience.

Without inventing responsibilities.

---

# AI Positioning

AI should never appear as "AI writes my code."

Instead, AI should be presented as part of my engineering workflow.

Preferred wording:

* improving engineering workflows
* accelerating development
* debugging
* testing
* documentation
* refactoring
* code review
* productivity

Architecture and technical decisions must always remain human-driven.

Avoid expressions like:

> engineering multiplier

because they sound AI-generated.

# Company Descriptions

Recruiters care much more about what I built than about what the company does.

Whenever possible:

Prefer concise project context.

Example:

Instead of:

> This company develops...

Prefer:

> Fintech platform focused on...

or

> Medical traceability platform...

The emphasis should remain on my contribution.

---

# Bullet Point Philosophy

Never describe tasks.

Describe engineering impact.

Every bullet should communicate one or more of:

* ownership
* technical decisions
* scalability
* maintainability
* architecture
* delivery
* engineering quality
* collaboration

---

# Humanization Rules

One of the main goals was removing AI writing patterns.

Avoid:

* repetitive sentence structures
* repetitive verbs
* "Owned" repeated everywhere
* overly polished corporate wording
* ChatGPT-like phrasing

The resume should sound like it was written manually by an experienced engineer.

# LinkedIn Philosophy

LinkedIn is NOT a resume.

The objective is different.

LinkedIn should make recruiters immediately understand:

Who I am.

My level.

My specialization.

My industries.

My engineering maturity.

The About section should feel more conversational than the resume while keeping technical depth.
